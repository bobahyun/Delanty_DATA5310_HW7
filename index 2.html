Exploring Kyoto, Japan Temples: Religious Affiliations and Wheelchair Accessibility


This interactive map explores temples in Kyoto, Japan, with a focus on religious affiliation and wheelchair accessibility. The CartoDB Positron tileset was utilized to provide a clean background ensuring that the map is easy to read and navigate. The marker cluster was used to keep the map uncluttered, and allowing users to explore specific areas in Kyoto to view temples that are heavily clustered. Color-coded icons representing different religious affiliations are expressive in identifying temples. The color-coded wheelchair icons indicate if the temple has wheelchair accessibility in dark blue and limited accessibility in light blue. To enhance accessibility, tooltips displays temple names in both Japanese and English and their religious affiliation. Additionally, the tag filter button makes the map more effective by enabling exploration based on religious categories. Overall this map is designed for cultural exploration, allowing users to learn more about the clustering of temples based on religion, with a focus on meeting the needs of those with mobility considerations.
